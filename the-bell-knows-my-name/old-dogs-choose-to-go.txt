[Verse 1 — slow, intimate, solo violin]
The gate was open half a breath — I swear I barely turned —
but the night had something old in it, and something in her yearned;
she didn't bolt, she wandered slow, like something calling low,
and I called until my voice gave out — but old dogs choose to go

[Pre-chorus — building, accordion swells in]
and the fiddle starts confessing
what my throat could never call

[Chorus — slow-frantic, full band, harmonic minor]
so whistle it low, whistle it breaking, let the lanterns comb the night
I was made to do the calling, she was made to chase the light
I've got a leash where my heart goes, got a silence where she'd heel
so tell me, someone, tell me — is the losing how we heal?

[Verse 2 — slow again]
They found her in the roadside mud the winter I turned nine,
half-wolf and all suspicion, and she never once was mine —
but she slept against my shaking and she growled away my fear;
I named her for my grandmother, and I kept her fourteen years

[Bridge — half-time, sparse, then huge]
maybe leaving's how they love us
maybe going's how they stay
maybe she just walked ahead of me
to keep the dark away
— (violin builds, tempo doubles) —
so sing her hooome—

[Final Chorus — biggest, gang vocals on the last line]
whistle it low, whistle it breaking, let the morning take the road
I was made to do the calling — and she carried half the load
I've got a leash where my heart goes, got a silence where she'd heel
and the whistle never found her — but God, she taught me how to feel
