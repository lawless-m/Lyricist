[Verse 1 — slow, intimate, solo violin]
I walked them through the same old gate my father walked me through
same rust along the railing, same grey the morning knew
but the gate don't know me anymore, the stones don't call me near —
I'm a stranger in the only place that ever kept me here

[Pre-chorus — building, accordion swells in]
and the fiddle starts confessing
that the stranger here is me

[Chorus — cinematic, sparse, ragged crowd]
so play it worn, play it faded, let the old bell ring them through
I'm the boy that grew a stranger, I'm the son the walls outgrew
I've got years where the yard should be, got a ghost where the boy should stand
so tell me, father, tell me — did it end this way for you?

[Verse 2 — slow again]
My father knew each flagstone by the sound it made in rain,
he taught me where the yard dipped low, he walked me every lane
they buried him one April and the schoolbell never stopped —
it rang the children in to class, it rang above the plot

[Bridge — half-time, sparse, then huge]
maybe home was never brick
maybe home was just his coat
maybe I'm the only thing
round here that ever broke
— (violin builds, tempo doubles) —
so ring it hooome—

[Final Chorus — biggest, gang vocals on the last line]
play it worn, play it faded, let the morning lead them through
they belong to it the way I did, the way my father knew
I've got years where the yard should be, got a ghost where the boy should stand
but the bell still knows my name, boys — so let it ring me home
