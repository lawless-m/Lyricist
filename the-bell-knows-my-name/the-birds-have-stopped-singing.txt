[Verse 1 — slow, intimate, rubato, solo violin]
There's a quiet on this ridge that wasn't here a year ago,
no lark at first light, no crow at dusk, nothing left to know.
I used to curse them for the noise before the sun was up —
I'd give the whole of my two ears now just to hear one call it enough.

[Pre-chorus — building, accordion swells in]
and the strings go quiet too,
for the words I'll never say

[Chorus — fast, frantic, full band]
hush it low, hush it holy, let the whole ridge go still with him
I gave the birds my grief, and the birds gave me their silence
some mornings I still think I came out ahead of that bargain
does the quiet keep better count than we do?

[Verse 2 — slow again]
My brother Toma kept the traps up on this ridge since before I could carry them myself,
the spring after they buried him, not one bird came back to spring the wire.
I walked his lines out of habit, empty-handed, every dawn,
and the quiet followed me home like something that wanted feeding.

[Bridge — half-time, sparse, then huge]
maybe the birds were never singing for the ridge
maybe they were singing for him
maybe the quiet's not an absence
maybe it's just what he sounds like now
— (violin builds, tempo doubles) —
so let them stay gone—

[Final Chorus — biggest, gang vocals on the last line]
hush it low, hush it holy, let the whole valley go still with him
I gave the birds my grief, and the birds gave me their silence
and some nights now I'd trade the whole quiet back for the noise
and some nights now I'd trade the whole quiet back for the noise
