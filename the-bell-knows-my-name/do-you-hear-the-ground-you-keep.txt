[Verse 1 — slow, intimate, solo violin]
They came with paper in the morning, they came with pegs and string,
red flags along the green lane where you used to stand and sing.
I watched them from the window and I never made a sound —
you don't fight the tide with weeping, but by God, I held my ground.

[Pre-chorus — building, accordion swells in]
and the fiddle starts confessing
what my mouth won't dare to say

[Chorus — darker, full band, no crowd]
so keen it low, keen it holy, let the evening take the field,
I was born of this old valley and I will not, will not yield.
I've got fences where my road was, I've got deeds where my dead should sleep —
so tell me, strangers, tell me — do you hear the ground you keep?

[Verse 2 — slow again]
My grandmother's grandmother lies buried by the ash,
where they're hammering the markers now, where they'll lay the road at last.
She taught me every hedgerow, every ford, every stone —
now the ones who never knew her name will call this ground their own.

[Bridge — half-time, sparse, then huge]
maybe land was never having,
maybe ground was never mine,
maybe we were only borrowed
by the swallow and the vine,
— (violin builds, tempo doubles) —
so hold it clooose—

[Final Chorus — biggest, but she's alone]
keen it low, keen it holy, let the morning find me here,
I was born of this old valley and I'm not about to disappear.
I've got fences where my road was, I've got deeds where my dead should sleep —
and there's no one left to sing it, so it's mine alone to keep.
