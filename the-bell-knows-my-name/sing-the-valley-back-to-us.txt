[Female Vocal]
[Verse 1 — slow, intimate, solo violin]
They came with paper in the morning, they came with pegs and string,
red across our own green lane where our mothers used to sing.

[Male Vocal]
And we did not raise a hand to them, and we did not raise a sound,
but we know the names of every stone, and they will not know this ground.

[Pre-Chorus — building, accordion swells in]
[Female Vocal]
and the fiddle starts confessing

[Male Vocal]
what our mouths won't dare to say

[Duet — full band, communal]
[Chorus]
so keen it low, keen it holy, let the evening take the field,
we were born of this old valley and we will not, will not yield.
we've got fences where our road was, we've got deeds where our dead should sleep,
so tell us, strangers, tell us — do you hear the ground you keep?

[Female Vocal]
[Verse 2 — slow]
Our grandmothers' grandmothers lie buried by the ash,
where they're hammering the markers now, where they'll lay the road at last.

[Male Vocal]
They taught us every hedgerow, every ford, every stone,
now the ones who never knew their names will call this ground their own.

[Bridge — half-time, sparse]
[Female Vocal]
maybe land was never having,
maybe ground was never ours,

[Male Vocal]
maybe we were only borrowed
by the swallow and the vine,

[Duet — rising]
but borrowed things get given back
to hands that know them well,
so sing it hooome—

[Duet — biggest, full gang vocals]
[Final Chorus]
keen it low, keen it holy, let the morning find us here,
we were born of this old valley and we are not going to disappear.
we've got fences where our road was, we've got deeds where our dead should sleep,
and there's a thousand left to sing it, so it's ours, it's ours to keep.
