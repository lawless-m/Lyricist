[Verse 1 — slow, intimate, solo violin]
They fenced the field my father worked, they torched the caravan,
they stamped a borrowed name on me they couldn't understand.
you can have the road, the roof, the mare — I'll hand them with a grin —
but the fiddle holds my grandmother, and she is not going in

[Pre-chorus — building, accordion swells in]
and the bow begins its swearing
what my throat's too proud to say

[Chorus — defiant, driving, full band]
so play it loud, play it bleeding, let it carry past the wire
I've got ash where my roof should be, I've got song where they want fire
you can raze the whole encampment but you'll never raze the tune —
so tell me, soldier, tell me — have you papers for a tune?

[Verse 2 — slow again]
My grandmother was seven when the first invasion came,
she buried it beneath the plum tree, wouldn't even say its name.
they burned the church, they burned the list of everyone they'd owned —
but the plum tree kept the secret, and the fiddle came back home

[Bridge — half-time, sparse, then huge]
maybe a home was never walls
maybe a home was never land
maybe it's the one thing left
that they can't pull from your hand
— (violin builds, tempo doubles) —
so let them cooome—

[Final Chorus — biggest, gang vocals on the last line]
play it loud, play it bleeding, let it carry past the wire
I was never made for kneeling — I was made of strings and fire
you can raze the whole encampment but you'll never raze the tune —
you'll have to take this violin from my cold dead hands, invader
