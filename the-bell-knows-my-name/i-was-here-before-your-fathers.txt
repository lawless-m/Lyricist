[Verse 1 — slow, low, seething, solo violin]
You measured out my morning with your theodolite and chain,
you drove your little markers where my mother knew my name.
I did not weep, I did not run, I did not look away —
I sharpened every quiet word I've swallowed to this day.

[Pre-chorus — building, accordion swells in]
and the fiddle stops its weeping,
and it starts to bare its teeth

[Chorus — hard, driving, full band]
so strike it hard, strike it screaming, let the fiddle draw its knife,
I was born of this old valley and I'll leave it with my life.
I've got fire where my grief was, I've got iron where I should kneel —
so tell me, strangers, tell me — is it mine or yours to steal?

[Verse 2 — slow again, venom under it]
My grandmother's grandmother put her hands into this ground,
she's the marrow of these hedgerows, she's the low root's aching sound.
You can hammer all your markers, you can pave it flat and grey —
but she'll rot your careful footings and she'll drag your roads away.

[Bridge — half-time, sparse, then huge]
no, the land was never having,
no, this ground was never sold,
you don't buy a thing with paper
that was bought with blood and bone —
— (violin builds, tempo doubles) —
so come and taaake—

[Final Chorus — biggest, feral, still alone]
strike it hard, strike it screaming, let the morning find me armed,
I was born of this old valley and I will not be disarmed.
I've got fire where my grief was, I've got iron where I should kneel —
and there's no one here to stop me, so it's mine alone to steal.
