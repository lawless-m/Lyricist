[Verse 1 — slow, intimate, solo violin, rubato]
I never buried anyone — I left them where they lay,
two branches for a headstone and a name I couldn't say.
The road won't stop for grieving and it never learned to wait,
so I've been the only churchyard where my family lies in state.
[Pre-chorus — building, accordion swells in]
and the violin remembers
every name I set down cold —
[Chorus — sparse, then swelling]
so dig it slow, dig it holy, let the whole road hold its breath,
I've got a hundred unmarked mornings and I owe them each a death.
I've got graves where my step should be, I've got ghosts where I should grieve —
so tell me, brother, tell me — do the dead forgive the ones who leave?
[Verse 2 — slow again]
My brother went to fever in a town I couldn't name,
I left him to the strangers and the cold October rain.
I swore I'd come back for him when the caravan was through —
but the caravan is never through, and neither, love, are you.
[Bridge — half-time, sparse, then huge]
maybe grief is just a country
that you cross but never leave,
maybe every mile's a rosary
strung with names you couldn't keep —
(violin builds, tempo doubles)
so sing them hooome—
[Final Chorus — biggest, gang vocals on the last line]
so dig it slow, dig it holy, let the whole road bow its head —
I've been my family's only churchyard and I'm running out of dead.
I've got graves where my step should be, I've got ghosts where I should grieve,
and the road never gave them back to me — but tonight, boys, let them leave.
