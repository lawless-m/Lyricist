[Verse 1 — slow, intimate, solo violin]
I put my hand against the door and the door put a hand on me
the paint had learned my father's face, the hinge remembered me
there's a window in the corridor shows nineteen-fifty-three —
my boy walks past, my father waves, and none of them is me

[Pre-chorus — building, accordion swells in]
and the fiddle starts confessing
that the school won't let us go

[Chorus — cinematic, sparse, ragged crowd]
so play it slow, play it sideways, let the old bell ring us through
I'm the ghost that walks it forward, I'm the son the halls walked to
I've got doors where the days should be, got a stair where the year should end
so tell me, father, tell me — does the building dream us too?

[Verse 2 — slow again]
My father's coat still walks the stairs on afternoons of rain,
I've seen it turn the corner where he taught me every lane
they buried him one April but the corridor forgot —
it keeps a version of him warm, a step that time can't stop

[Bridge — half-time, sparse, then huge]
maybe home was never now
maybe home was every when
maybe I'm the seam it folds
to fit the boys back in
— (violin builds, tempo doubles) —
so ring us hooome—

[Final Chorus — biggest, gang vocals on the last line]
play it slow, play it sideways, let the morning walk us through
they belong to it, I belong to it, my father belongs to it too
I've got doors where the days should be, got a stair where the year should end
but the bell still knows my name, boys — so ring the whole line home
