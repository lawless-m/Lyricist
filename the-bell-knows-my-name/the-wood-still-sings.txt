[Verse 1 — slow, intimate, solo violin]
This fiddle isn't mine, I only carry where it's been
the varnish holds your fingerprints, the rosin holds your skin
I tuck it to my collarbone the way you used to stand
and something in the maple wakes and plays without my hand

[Pre-chorus — building, accordion swells in]
and the bow says what I couldn't
at the edge of your last day

[Chorus — fast, frantic, full band]
so bow it low, bow it aching, let the graveyard hear it climb
I'm the last hand on the horsehair, I'm the keeper of your time
I've got hymns where my heart goes, I've got ghosts in every string
so tell me, father, tell me — is a song the only place you sing?

[Verse 2 — slow again]
You pressed this fiddle in my hands the night the fever won,
your knuckles white as candlewax, your borrowed breathing done —
you said the tunes outlast the men, the men were only loaned,
now every note's a room you're in, and none of them are home

[Bridge — half-time, sparse, then huge]
maybe death was never silence
maybe silence was the fear
maybe everyone we've buried
is the reason strings can hear
— (violin builds, tempo doubles) —
so call them baaack—

[Final Chorus — biggest, gang vocals on the last line]
bow it low, bow it aching, let the morning catch us here
I'm the last hand on the horsehair, and the dead are standing near
I've got hymns where my heart goes, I've got ghosts in every string
and the grave don't answer letters — but by God, the wood still sings
