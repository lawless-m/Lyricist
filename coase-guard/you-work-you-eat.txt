[Intro — cold industrial, spoken low, sparse]
Ask me what's for dinner. Ask me what you did today first.

[Verse — Quartermaster, cold-warm, sparse, more space between hits]
Table's not a right, it's a receipt.
You want the receipt, you gotta bring the day.
Chop the wood, mind the baby, mend the fence, sweep the floor —
don't care which, care that one of them got done.
The office called again, said "no child goes without,"
said it like a fact, said it like it's free.
Honey, nothing's free. I've never once seen free.
I've seen tired hands get fed, and I've seen the rest wait.

[Hook — chanted, sparser, colder]
You work, you eat. That's the whole of the law.
Not the office, not the form, not the good intentions in the hall.
Warm plate for a warm hand — that's the only faith I keep.
Love's not what gets you seated. Work is what gets you fed.

[Verse 2 — escalation, concrete detail]
Youngest one cried at the table, said it isn't fair,
said his brother worked the field and he just sat there.
I said good — that's the first true thing you've said all year.
Tomorrow you'll carry water, and tomorrow you'll eat here.
The office called back, called my rule "concerning,"
asked was I withholding care from a minor in the home.
I said one thing back: this house doesn't starve anyone.
It just doesn't feed anyone for free. Not one of them. Not me.

[The tally — spoken, flat]
Two hours at the woodpile earns one hour at my table. That's not strict. That's arithmetic.
I don't round up for tears.

[Final hook — chanted, weightier, colder]
You work, you eat. That's the whole of the law.
Not the office, not the form, not the good intentions in the hall.
WARM PLATE FOR A WORKING HAND — that's the only faith I keep.
THIS HOUSE FEEDS EVERY HAND THAT EARNED IT — AND NOT ONE THAT DIDN'T.
