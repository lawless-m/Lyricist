[Intro — cold, mechanized pulse, spoken low]
The tiger doesn't knock. It doesn't need to — nothing's left with a door.

[Verse 1 — clipped, technical, controlled]
I built the arches that held the roof up, cut the stone myself, set it true,
now the roof's a memory and the arches hold up nothing but the view.
They didn't burn it out of hatred. Worse — they never asked what it was for.
Paved it flat and called it progress, filed the ruins under "before."

[Hook — chanted, stacked]
So I climbed on. Didn't fight it. Didn't kneel down in the dust.
You don't out-run what's already running — you learn to ride, or you rust.
The ground I stood on isn't ground now, it's just weather with a name,
so I'm the man still standing upright, and the standing is the claim.

[Verse 2 — rapped, escalation]
My old master died believing someone still would need the trade,
left me his level and his calipers, the debts he never paid.
I use them now for balance, not for building — nothing left to build,
just the shape of what he taught me, holding steady while it's killed.

[The wink — spoken, flat, cold]
I still set my feet the way he showed me. Even here. Even now. Nobody's watching. I do it anyway.

[Final hook — chanted, weightier, full band]
So I climbed on. Didn't fight it. Didn't kneel down in the dust.
YOU DON'T OUT-RUN WHAT'S ALREADY RUNNING — YOU LEARN TO RIDE, OR YOU RUST.
The ground I stood on isn't ground now, it's just ashes with my name,
and I'm the man still standing upright — the standing is the only claim.
