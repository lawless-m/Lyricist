[Intro — cold, mechanized pulse, spoken low]
The car already knows the route. It's never once asked me where I actually want to go.

[Verse 1 — clipped, technical, controlled]
Hands in my lap at sixty, wheel turning without my hand,
the nav already knows the fastest way to everywhere I've ever planned.
It reads the traffic minutes before the light even turns red —
knows more about my next ten minutes than I know about the years ahead.

[Hook — chanted, stacked]
It knows every bend in the road. It's never once asked me why.
Optimized the route to the minute — nobody optimized what for.
I let go of the wheel so long ago I don't remember letting go.
The miles keep adding up. Nothing's adding up to a place I know.

[Verse 2 — rapped, escalation]
My ex schedules the handoff through an app now, not a phone call anymore,
the car already knows the address before I tell it what the trip's for.
My daughter asks "are we there yet" out of habit — the dash answers before she's done asking.
I used to know the way by the charging stations. Now I don't know the way at all.

[The wink — spoken, flat, cold]
I typed home into the app last Tuesday. It asked which one.

[Final hook — chanted, weightier, full band]
It knows every bend in the road. IT'S NEVER ONCE ASKED ME WHY.
Optimized the route to the minute — nobody optimized what for.
I let go of the wheel so long ago I don't remember letting go.
The miles keep adding up. Nothing's adding up to a place I know.
