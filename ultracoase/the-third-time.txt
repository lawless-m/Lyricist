[Intro — cold, mechanized pulse, spoken low]
The sea doesn't remember it's done this before. We do.

[Verse 1 — clipped, technical, controlled]
Counted eleven sails from the headland, same as my father counted before,
same wind, same tide, same stretch of grey water bringing the same thing to shore.
We still know the old speeches — the few-against-many kind, the ones that turn a tide,
the ones written for a beach that, that one time, the enemy never even tried.
But the elders had already met by the time I climbed back down —
door bolted, terms decided, nothing left to defend but the sound.

[Hook — chanted, stacked]
This isn't the first time the water's had its way with this coast,
it won't be the last time either — that's the part that costs the most.
Third time the sea's come calling. Third time we let it in.
Standing here is not surviving — standing here is where I've always been.

[Verse 2 — rapped, escalation]
My father told me on this headland it was the last time, watching men land,
he was wrong the first time he said it, and wrong again second-hand.
I won't tell my own son it's over. I'll just teach him how to count the sails,
how to climb down before they see him, how the old stories always fail.

[The wink — spoken, flat, cold]
I counted the sails again this morning. Eleven, same as always. I didn't go down to the shore. I never do.

[Final hook — chanted, weightier, full band]
This isn't the first time the water's had its way with this coast,
IT WON'T BE THE LAST TIME EITHER — THAT'S THE PART THAT COSTS THE MOST.
Third time the sea's come calling. Third time we let it in.
Standing here is not surviving — standing here is where I've always been.
