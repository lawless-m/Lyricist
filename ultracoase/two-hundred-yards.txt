[Intro — cold, spoken, mechanized pulse]
A gate doesn't fail because of one bad storm. It fails because of one skipped check, years back, that nobody ever went back and fixed. I still walk mine every night before I sleep, storm or not.

[Verse 1 — clipped, technical, controlled]
Sixteen locks on this stretch, mine's the only one still logged by hand,
paddle count, gate check, water level — same three things, same order, every stand.
I've not missed a single one since before the depot changed hands,
the others call it superstition. I call it the only thing that's ever held.

[Hook — chanted, stacked]
Nobody drowns from the storm that's coming — they drown from the gate somebody left half-shut.
I don't check it because I trust the water, I check it because the water doesn't care if I do.
Sixteen locks on this stretch and only one still gets walked every night.
I'd walk it alone forever, if that's what it costs to keep it standing.

[The wink — spoken, flat, cold]
My sister's cottage sat two hundred yards past his gate, not mine. She got out with the dog and nothing else.
I checked the distance after. Like it would have made a difference.

[Verse 2 — rapped, escalation]
He'd stopped logging his checks long before it happened, said the water always sorts itself out,
I sent him my sheet every quarter anyway — he never sent his back.
Nobody made him rebuild the gate right, nobody made him check it either,
I just kept walking mine. Same order, same three things, every night.

[Final hook — chanted, weightier, full band]
Nobody drowns from the storm that's coming — they drown from the gate somebody left half-shut.
TWO HUNDRED YARDS PAST HIS GATE, NOT MINE. I CHECKED THE DISTANCE AFTER.
Sixteen locks on this stretch and only one still gets walked every night.
I'll walk it alone forever, if that's what it costs to keep it standing.
