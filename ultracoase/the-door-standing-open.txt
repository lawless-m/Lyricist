[Verse 1 — clipped, technical, controlled]
Freeboard's down to eleven inches at spring tide, has been for six years running,
filed the same report every March: patch the toe now or lose it by autumn.
They funded the offset fund instead, bought credits from a country we've never traded with,
called it leadership. I called it a number on a wall nobody read.

[The wink — spoken, flat, cold]
I trained him on that stretch of wall myself.
He went out to sandbag the toe the night it went. I signed the roster that put him there.

[Verse 2 — rapped, escalation]
Inquiry called it a freak surge, called it unprecedented, called it nobody's fault,
I've got six years of reports that say otherwise, stamped, dated, filed, ignored.
I still walk that stretch every dawn and still measure the same eleven inches,
they're pouring the new wall now, paid for, finally, the week after.

[Hook — chanted, stacked]
We paid for the sky and left the door standing open,
bought ourselves a headline while the water found the gap.
Every inch I measured was a warning, never once a metaphor,
and I'll measure the same eleven inches every March, and I'd file it again.

[Final hook — chanted, weightier, full band]
We paid for the sky and left the door standing open,
ELEVEN INCHES WAS THE WARNING. ELEVEN INCHES WAS THE PRICE.
Every inch I measured was a warning, never once a metaphor,
and I'll measure the same eleven inches every March, and I'll file it again.
