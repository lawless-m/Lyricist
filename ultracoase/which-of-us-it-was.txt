[Intro — cold, spoken, mechanized pulse]
The dawn chorus used to wake me before the alarm did. Some mornings now I sleep straight through it. I can't tell you if that's because the birds stopped, or because I did.

[Verse 1 — clipped, technical, controlled]
Same six hundred metres every May since eighty-six, same hour, same hedge line,
thirty-seven species logged by ear the first year, name each one before it finishes the phrase.
Warbler's the first to go quiet in my head — too high, too fast, gone before I've placed it,
I write "possible" next to it now. I never used to write possible.

[Hook — chanted, stacked]
I can't tell if the hedge went quiet or my ears did — the audiogram says both are true.
Thirty-seven species logged the year I started. Twelve, most mornings, now.
Some of those twelve I'm only guessing at the song, not hearing it, guessing.
I'll walk the same six hundred metres every May, and I still won't know which of us it was.

[Verse 2 — rapped, escalation]
Sent the twelve-year data to the trust, they said the trend's real, said it's not just me,
sent them my audiogram anyway, said mark it against the count, be honest about both lines.
They didn't call back about that part. Nobody wants two variables in one graph.
I keep walking it either way — can't fix the hedgerow, might as well keep the notebook honest.

[The wink — spoken, flat, cold]
My wife walks the transect behind me most mornings now, twenty minutes back, same route.
I've never asked her what she hears that I don't. She's never told me.
