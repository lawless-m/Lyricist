[Intro — cold, spoken, mechanized pulse]
The blade that cuts a graft and the blade that clears a boundary line are the same length, the same edge. Some years, so was the reason we picked it up.

[Verse 1 — clipped, technical, controlled]
Whip and tongue, wax the cut, bind it tight before it dries —
two different woods will knit as one if you match the cambium lines.
Twenty years we ran this row together, his rootstock, my scion wood,
half these trees don't remember whose hand started them, and I liked it that way, understood.

[Verse 2 — rapped, escalation]
It came apart in three days, not the thirty years we'd built it,
men came up through the lower road, same blades we used on wood — just not that week.
He knocked before light, said take what won't wait, I filled his tank, I filled his bag,
told myself I'd follow once my own gate was locked. I'm still telling myself that.

[Hook — chanted, stacked]
Two woods knit close in the same spring rain, same ring, same rising sap —
grows as one tree in the calm, splits along the graft when the weather turns.
Root never asked the scion's name. Bark never learned to choose a side.
I'd bind the wound again every March, and watch it open again every time.

[The wink — spoken, flat, cold]
I told him to take my truck. I didn't go with them as far as the crossing.
My side of the valley stayed quiet that week.

[Final hook — chanted, weightier, full band]
Two woods knit close in the same spring rain, same ring, same rising sap —
GROWS AS ONE TREE IN THE CALM. SPLITS ALONG THE GRAFT WHEN THE WEATHER TURNS.
His half of the orchard still fruits every year. Nobody left to pick it.
and I'd bind the wound again every March, but the frost always finds the line.
