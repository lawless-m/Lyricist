[Intro — cold, mechanized pulse, spoken low]
Nobody rang the bell when I left. The forge doesn't wait for anyone — least of all me.

[Verse 1 — clipped, technical, controlled]
Sold the hammer for a ticket, sold it before I could think,
told the buyer it was heavy — didn't tell him where it sinks.
Weight's still in my shoulder. Weight's still in my walk.
Some things don't leave the body just because you stopped the talk.

[Hook — chanted, stacked]
Strike it once, strike it patient — that's the only law I broke
I know the weight of the hammer, not the color of the flame
Brother kept the fire lit. Brother kept the name.
I traded both for distance, and I'd do it all again.

[Verse 2 — rapped, escalation]
Father died beside the anvil, tongs still in his hand,
brother closed his eyes for him — I was two states off the land.
I send the money monthly. Call it penance, call it rent.
He sends a photograph of smoke. That's the only word he's sent.

[The wink — spoken, flat, cold]
...and yeah — I tell people I left for the work.
Truth is I left because staying meant becoming him.
Doesn't make the leaving right. Just makes it mine to carry.

[Final hook — chanted, weightier, full band]
Strike it once, strike it patient — that's the only law I broke
I know the weight of the hammer, not the color of the flame
THE FIRE KEPT GOING WITHOUT ME. THE FIRE DIDN'T NEED MY NAME.
and the leaving didn't save me, but the leaving wears my name.
