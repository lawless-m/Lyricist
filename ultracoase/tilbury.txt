[Intro — cold, mechanized pulse, spoken low]
Tilbury, 1588. She told an army she had the heart of a king. Nobody checked if it was true. It just needed saying.

[Verse 1 — clipped, technical, controlled]
Stood in front of an army in borrowed armor, over four hundred years back,
told a field of men she had a lion's heart under a woman's back.
Didn't ask the room's permission. Didn't wait to see who'd agree.
Said it once, in the open, in front of everyone who could see.

[Hook — chanted, stacked]
She said it once. The field went still.
Mine's loaded. I'm choosing the ground.
Heart of a king costs nothing to claim —
the stomach picks the moment, not the crowd.

[Verse 2 — rapped, escalation]
My father called it something once, in a meeting, out loud, and lost the room for it,
wore the name they gave him after like a coat, and never once tried to be rid of it.
I've had the same sentence loaded for a decade, keeping the powder dry —
waiting for the ground where it lands as a fact, and not a firework in the sky.

[The wink — spoken, flat, cold]
I've drafted it nine times. I keep the ninth, not the first. I haven't sent any of them yet.

[Final hook — chanted, weightier, full band]
She said it once. The field went still.
MINE'S LOADED. I'M CHOOSING THE GROUND.
Heart of a king costs nothing to claim —
the stomach picks the moment, not the crowd.
