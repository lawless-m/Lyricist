[Verse 1 — fast, clipped, flat, sampler stabs]
Got a black shape at the foot of the bed, it answers when I call
I feed it twice, it sheds the dark on everything I own
there's the dog and there's the other one and both of them are black
one heels when I say heel, the other heels a beat behind
she watches from the doorway with the porch light in her eyes
I've been walking something nightly and I've stopped checking which
and I'm not even down. The black just gets more evenly spread.

[Pre-hook — sampler grabs a word, chops]
which one— which one— (which)— (—one)
— and the low end starts to pace —

[Hook — flat chant, gang shout on the last word]
so walk it, heel it, hold the dark, stay
I can't tell which black dog I'm walking —
(gang) good dog, good dog
she loves me like it costs her nothing and I can't love it back the same
and which of us is on the lead — / the leash goes slack both ways
good dog. good dog.

[Verse 2 — clipped, image-stream, non-sequitur]
Black on the sofa, black on the coat, black working into the grain,
I vacuum up a version of her and she fills back in by noon.

I've been the owner my whole life. The owning went both ways.
The black doesn't wash. It's in the water now. It comes out the tap—
