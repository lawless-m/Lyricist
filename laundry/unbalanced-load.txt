[Verse 1 — slow, half-spoken, muttered, sludgy lurching rhythm]
Watching the drum go round through the fogged glass porthole.
Same red sock in there for four cycles now, never coming out clean, never coming out.
The machine's already learned my shape — off-balance in the same corner every time.
I stand here longer than the cycle needs. I don't know why I still do that.

[Pre-hook — sampler grabs the word "round", chops it, drags it; both kits compress and thicken at half speed]
round / round / (rou—) / (—nd)

[Hook — half-sung, ranted delivery, gang backing at half speed]
spin it out, wring it dry, fold what's left, forget the rest
error code UE, unbalanced load, it knows before I do
(gang) spin it out, fold what's left
it's been four loads and I still haven't opened the mail
wash, rinse, repeat
wash, rinse, repeat

[Verse 2 — muttered, image-stream, non-sequitur]
Static cling. My own sock stuck to my sleeve like it's trying to leave with me.
Fabric softener smell in a colour that doesn't exist outside the bottle.
Lint trap full of a year I can't account for — grey, matted, none of it mine exactly.
Machine shudders on the spin. Corrects itself. Keeps going anyway.

[Hook — identical words, gang backing]
spin it out, wring it dry, fold what's left, forget the rest
error code UE, unbalanced load, it knows before I do
(gang) spin it out, fold what's left
it's been four loads and I still haven't opened the mail
wash, rinse, repeat
wash, rinse, repeat

[The dissolve — spoken, mock-composed, thinning, then chopped and looped into the sampler, dragged to half speed]
I can hear it finding its balance, I can hear it — I can h—
(h-hear) / (h-it) / (—find)

[No scream. Just the spin cycle, correcting itself, and the last of the water draining out.]

[Final hook — biggest, whole room, mantra dragged, pitched and degraded]
spin it out, wring it dry, fold what's left, forget the rest
and the sock never comes back and neither does whatever the sock was standing in for
(gang, smeared) spin it out, fold what's left
wash, rinse — wash, rinse — wash—

[Loop left running, faded, no ending]
