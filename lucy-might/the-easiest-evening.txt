[Verse 1 — piano and upright bass only, brushes barely there, room tone. Voice very close, low, warm, unhurried, behind the beat. She is answering something that was never said out loud.]
You've not tried once all night.
Do you know how rare that is in here.
You asked about the drive. You asked if I was cold.
Nobody's asked me if I was cold since nineteen ninety.

And I've been sat here doing the arithmetic
for the first time from this side of the table.
That's new. I'll be honest with you, that's new.
Don't do anything with it.

[Pre-hook — bass walks, one muted trumpet steps into the room. Small lift. Not a build.]
I've never wanted it so much
and you've not even noticed you've done anything.

[Hook — barely bigger. Low, flat, warm, unhurried.]
don't say anything clever
you've been getting it right for two hours by accident
I've never wanted it so much
and it's never mattered less what I want
you're the easiest evening I've had
and you'll never know you were

[Verse 2 — vibraphone in, very soft. Nothing else changes.]
You want me to say you're restful.
Not handsome, not dangerous — restful.
That being sat near you is the pleasant part of somebody's week.
That's the order. I knew it before the coats.

And you are. God help you, you are.
Twenty years of people performing at me
and you asked about the traffic on the A6
and I nearly told you something true.

Your hands have been on the table the whole time.
Not doing anything. Just there. Warm.
Your eyes went to the mouth once and came straight back up,
and that's the only thing you've done all night that you'd apologise for.

[Hook — identical words. Do not change them. The verse changed them for you.]
don't say anything clever
you've been getting it right for two hours by accident
I've never wanted it so much
and it's never mattered less what I want
you're the easiest evening I've had
and you'll never know you were

[The near-yes — everything drops. Brushes and one muted trumpet, very quiet. She goes closer, warmer, softer, until the song appears to arrive. This is the closest the catalogue ever gets.]
Closer.
Closer than that.
Close enough to smell the soap. That's all it is. Soap.
And I'd have stayed. That's what I'm telling you.

[She takes her keys off the table and stands. Slower than usual. The band comes back in underneath her, gently, before he has worked out that anything happened at all.]

[Final hook — one word changed. Low, warm, no amusement in it this time, walking away. Hips are the last thing anyone sees.]
don't say anything clever
you've been getting it right for two hours by accident
I've never wanted it so much
and it's never mattered less what I want
you were the easiest evening I've had
and you'll never know you were

[Outro — the trumpet takes a phrase and doesn't finish it. Band plays on for another table, quieter than it has ever played. No resolving chord, no ending.]
