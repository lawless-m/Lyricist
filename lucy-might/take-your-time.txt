[Verse 1 — noir big band held right down. Muted brass, low strings, upright bass, brushed kit. Voice close, low, warm, behind the beat, never belting. She is answering something that was never said out loud.]
No, don't apologise. I liked it.
You looked. Then you looked away. Then you looked.
That's the good version.
Most people only manage the first one.

You said you used to be somebody in this town.
Used to be. You said it like a confession.
I've heard confessions in here.
That one's not a confession.

[Pre-hook — brass swells one notch, bass walks, strings underneath. Small lift. Not a build.]
You did the legs. All the way up. Twice.
And I let the sentence go.

[Hook — barely bigger. Low, flat, warm, unhurried. Solo vocal.]
take your time
the night's not in a hurry and neither am I
I'm older than you think
and you'd have said yes anyway
I've been called a lot of things across this table
never licked

[Verse 2 — vibraphone and one clarinet added, nothing else changes.]
You want me to say you've still got it.
That's the order. I knew it at the door.
And here's the part you won't believe —
you have. You do. I wouldn't have sat down.

Your hands remembered something while you were talking.
Went to the tie, went to the glass, went nowhere.
And your eyes did the room, and came back, and stayed.
Go on. That's the compliment. The rest is just noise.

[Hook — identical words. Do not change them. The verse changed them for you.]
take your time
the night's not in a hurry and neither am I
I'm older than you think
and you'd have said yes anyway
I've been called a lot of things across this table
never licked

[The near-yes — the whole band drops out. Brushes and one muted trumpet, very quiet. She goes closer, warmer, softer, until the song appears to arrive.]
Closer.
Closer than that.
Close enough to smell the gin go warm.
You've still got it. You've always had it.

[She picks her lighter up off the table and stands. The band comes back in underneath her — brass first — before he has worked out what happened.]

[Final hook — one word changed. Low, amused, entirely warm, walking away. Hips are the last thing anyone sees.]
take your time
the night's not in a hurry and neither am I
I'm older than you think
and you'd have said yes anyway
I've been called a lot of things across this table
never licked
you took your time

[Outro — the trumpet takes a phrase and doesn't finish it. Band plays on for another table. No resolving chord, no ending.]
