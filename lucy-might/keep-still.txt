[Verse 1 — piano, upright bass, brushes barely there, muted trumpet down a corridor. Voice very close, low, warm, unhurried, behind the beat. She is answering something that was never said out loud.]
Keep still.
No, I'm not doing anything. I'm just saying it.
You flinched when the lad went past with the tray
and you've been sat like that since half past eight.

New shirt. It's still got the folds in.
Somebody ironed a shirt to come and sit in a bar.
Don't look like that, I think it's the best thing I've seen all week.
You've made an effort at a room that's never made one at you.

[Pre-hook — bass walks, one muted trumpet steps into the room. Small lift. Not a build.]
Nobody's put a hand anywhere near you in a long time,
and you'd not have asked, and you weren't going to.

[Hook — barely bigger. Low, flat, warm, unhurried. Solo vocal.]
keep still
you've been braced for four hours and nothing's coming
my fingers on your neck
and I've not moved out of this chair
have that for the week
you'll not need to tell anybody

[Verse 2 — vibraphone in, very soft. Nothing else changes.]
You want me to say you're worth being gentle with.
Not wanted — that's not the order, that's the cheap one.
Gentle. That somebody could be careful with you on purpose
and not be doing it out of pity or a job.

And you are, and they could, and it's a scandal that nobody has.
Twenty-odd years I've watched people come in here starving
and about one in a hundred's starving for that,
and they're the only ones I ever think about on the way home.

Your hands have been round that glass since you sat down.
Your eyes did the fingers. Only the fingers.
Not the mouth, not the rest, just the fingers, for four hours.
I know what you were looking at. I've known all night.

[Hook — identical words. Do not change them. The verse changed them for you.]
keep still
you've been braced for four hours and nothing's coming
my fingers on your neck
and I've not moved out of this chair
have that for the week
you'll not need to tell anybody

[The near-yes — everything drops. Brushes and one muted trumpet, very quiet. She goes closer, warmer, softer, until the song appears to arrive. She does not touch him at any point.]
Closer.
Closer than that.
Close enough to smell the shop still on your collar.
There. That's where they'd have gone. Just there.

[She takes her glass off the table and stands. The band comes back in underneath her before he has worked out that nothing touched him.]

[Final hook — one word changed. Low, warm, walking away. Hips are the last thing anyone sees.]
keep still
you've been braced for four hours and nothing's coming
my fingers on your neck
and I never moved out of this chair
have that for the week
you'll not need to tell anybody

[Outro — the trumpet takes a phrase and doesn't finish it. Band plays on for another table. No resolving chord, no ending.]
