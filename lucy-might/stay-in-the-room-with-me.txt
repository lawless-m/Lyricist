[Verse 1 — noir big band held right down. Muted brass, low strings, upright bass, brushed kit. Voice close, low, warm, behind the beat, never belting. She is answering something that was never said out loud.]
You don't need imagination. It's all here.
Every last bit of it, on this side of the table,
in the light, at eye level, for four hours,
and you've spent the evening picturing something else.

That's the bit I've never understood about any of you.
There's a whole woman here talking.
And you've gone somewhere private to think about her.
Come back. It's better out here.

[Pre-hook — brass steps in one notch, strings underneath, bass walks. Small lift. Not a build.]
You looked at the bosom and then went away for a second.
I watched you leave. Where do you go?

[Hook — barely bigger. Low, flat, warm, unhurried. Solo vocal.]
open your eyes
whatever you've built in there, I promise it's worse
it's all here
and nobody's ever finished looking
stay in the room with me
that's the only thing I've ever asked anyone

[Verse 2 — vibraphone and one muted trombone added. Strings hold. Nothing else changes.]
You want me to say it's not shallow. The looking.
That's the order — I knew it when the eyes came up guilty.
That a man can want the whole obvious thing
and not be a lesser man for it.

You're not. It's the plainest hunger there is
and there's nothing underneath it and that's fine,
and if you'd stop apologising for two minutes
you'd have had a much better evening.

Your hands stayed exactly where you put them all night.
Very good. Very disciplined. Nobody asked you to be.
Your eyes did the legs and came up quick like a schoolboy,
and I'd have let you take your time. I did say.

[Hook — identical words. Do not change them. The verse changed them for you.]
open your eyes
whatever you've built in there, I promise it's worse
it's all here
and nobody's ever finished looking
stay in the room with me
that's the only thing I've ever asked anyone

[The near-yes — everything drops. Brushes and one muted trumpet, very quiet. She goes closer, warmer, softer, until the song appears to arrive.]
Closer.
Closer than that.
Close enough to smell the perfume off the bottle on the bar.
Nothing in your head was ever going to be this near.

[She takes her bag off the back of the chair and stands. The band comes back in underneath her — strings first — before he has worked out what he missed.]

[Final hook — one word changed. Low, warm, amused, walking away. Hips are the last thing anyone sees.]
open your eyes
whatever you've built in there, I promise it's worse
it was all here
and nobody's ever finished looking
stay in the room with me
that's the only thing I ever asked anyone

[Outro — the trumpet takes a phrase and doesn't finish it. Band plays on for another table. No resolving chord, no ending.]
