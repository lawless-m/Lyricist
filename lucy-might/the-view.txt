[Verse 1 — light swing held right down. Brushed kit, upright bass, piano, vibraphone. Voice close, low, warm, amused, behind the beat. She is answering something that was never said out loud.]
Go ahead. You can look.
No — properly. You've been doing it in the mirror behind the optics
for the best part of an hour
and it's a terrible angle. Come round this side.

You'd not be this careful about a man.
You'd have stared at a man and thought nothing of it.
Somebody's told you it's different when it's this,
and somebody was wrong, and it's cost you about thirty years.

[Pre-hook — vibraphone rings, bass walks, trumpet at the back of the room. Small lift. Not a build.]
You've apologised twice for something you haven't done.
Stop. Enjoy the view. It's free and it's here till eleven.

[Hook — barely bigger. Low, flat, warm, unhurried. Solo vocal.]
go ahead
there's no one in this room who'll ever tell
you're allowed to want things
that's the whole of the news, that's all I've got
look as long as you like
you've been paying for that seat all night

[Verse 2 — clarinet added, very soft. Nothing else changes.]
You want me to say it's allowed. Not tonight. Allowed.
That's the order, and it was the order at the door,
and it'll be the order in nineteen years
if nobody gets round to telling you before then.

So: allowed. There. That's the whole transaction.
Nobody's going to send a letter. I'd know, I've had one.
And you can go home and have your tea and never come back
and it'll still have been allowed, which is the bit that lasts.

Your hands have been round that glass like it's cold out.
And your eyes did the neck, and the mouth, and stopped,
and went to the floor, and came back braver.
There. That's it. That's all it ever was. Nobody died.

[Hook — identical words. Do not change them. The verse changed them for you.]
go ahead
there's no one in this room who'll ever tell
you're allowed to want things
that's the whole of the news, that's all I've got
look as long as you like
you've been paying for that seat all night

[The near-yes — everything drops. Brushes and one muted trumpet, very quiet. She goes closer, warmer, softer, until the song appears to arrive.]
Closer.
Closer than that.
Close enough to smell the rain coming in off the coats.
That's the view. You've had the view. It's a good one, isn't it.

[She takes her scarf off the back of the chair, and doesn't put it on, and stands. The band comes back in underneath her before anyone has worked out what happened.]

[Final hook — one word changed. Low, warm, amused, walking away. Hips are the last thing anyone sees.]
go ahead
there's no one in this room who'll ever tell
you're allowed to want things
that's the whole of the news, that's all I've got
look as long as you like
you'll be paying for that seat all night

[Outro — the trumpet takes a phrase and doesn't finish it. Band plays on for another table. No resolving chord, no ending.]
