[Verse 1 — piano and upright bass only, brushes barely there, room tone. Voice very close, low, warm, unhurried, behind the beat. She is answering something that was never said out loud.]
I know. You've been working out how to ask
since the second I sat down.
You did the arithmetic and you liked the answer.
Everybody does the arithmetic.

You said nobody's ever really tried.
Nobody's ever really tried.
That's not the part you got wrong,
but it's a lovely place to start.

[Pre-hook — bass walks, one muted trumpet steps into the room. Small lift. Not a build.]
Your eyes went down and took the long way back
and I gave you the time.

[Hook — barely bigger. Low, flat, warm, no crowd, no backing vocals.]
don't get up
we've got all night and I've got nowhere
I have wanted to. Twice. In nineteen years.
you could be the first —
nobody's licked me yet
nobody's licked me yet

[Verse 2 — vibraphone added, nothing else changes.]
You want to be the one it finally happened with.
Not the evening. The story afterwards.
The one who got there. The exception.
I've poured that drink a thousand times.

Your hands have been very still.
Your fingers on the glass, going round and round.
And the calves, and back up, and the mouth, and away.
Go on. It's the best part of my night.

[Hook — identical words. Do not change them. The verse changed them for you.]
don't get up
we've got all night and I've got nowhere
I have wanted to. Twice. In nineteen years.
you could be the first —
nobody's licked me yet
nobody's licked me yet

[The near-yes — everything drops. Brushes and one muted trumpet, very quiet. She goes closer, warmer, softer, until the song appears to arrive.]
Closer.
Closer than that.
Close enough to smell where the perfume stops.
And you're still not the first.

[She reaches past him for her glass, finishes it, and stands. The band comes back in underneath her before he has worked out what happened.]

[Final hook — one word changed. Low, amused, entirely warm, walking away, hips last.]
don't get up
we've got all night and I've got nowhere
I have wanted to. Twice. In nineteen years.
you could have been the first —
nobody's licked me yet
nobody's licked me yet

[Outro — the trumpet takes a phrase and doesn't finish it. Band plays on for another table. No resolving chord, no ending.]
