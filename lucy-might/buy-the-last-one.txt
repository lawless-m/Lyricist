[Verse 1 — piano, upright bass, brushes barely there, muted trumpet down a corridor. Voice close, low, warm, unhurried, behind the beat. She is answering something that was never said out loud.]
I woke up naked.
On my own. At about four.
Nothing had happened. Nothing ever does.
That's the entire scandal, and you've gone red.

That's what you wanted to know, though, isn't it.
Not the evening. What comes after the evening.
Whether the room carries on when you're not in it.
It does. It's very quiet. You'd hate it.

[Pre-hook — bass walks, trumpet steps one pace nearer. Small lift. Not a build.]
You've been rehearsing a question since the second drink
and it's a better question than you think it is.

[Verse 2 — vibraphone in, very soft. Nothing else changes.]
You want me to say you'll still be here at four.
Not tonight — that. That's the order.
That somebody who's had a career's worth of evenings
kept one. Kept yours. That's what's being asked.

And somebody is kept. I'll give you that much and no more,
because the not-knowing is the good bit and I'd not spoil it.
You'd be no use to me certain.
Nobody's ever been any use to me certain.

Your hands went to the phone and back three times.

and then the clock behind me, which is the only honest thing
anybody's looked at all night.
