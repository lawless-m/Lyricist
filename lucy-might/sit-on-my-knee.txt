[Verse 1 — piano, upright bass, brushes barely there, muted trumpet down a corridor. Voice close, low, warm, unhurried, behind the beat. She is answering something that was never said out loud.]
You've been stood at the end of that bar for an hour
deciding whether you're allowed.
And you've had your hair done. Don't. Don't do that with your face.
You've had your hair done and I noticed and that's all I said.

Sit on my knee, pretty lady,
and let your troubles melt away.
No, they won't. I've never seen it work.
But sit down anyway.

[Pre-hook — bass walks, trumpet steps one pace nearer. Small lift. Not a build.]
You keep looking at the door like it's going to ask you something.
It isn't. I am.

[Hook — barely bigger. Low, flat, warm, unhurried. Solo vocal.]
sit on my knee
you got dressed for this and somebody ought to say so
you're not late
you're just the last one to be told
and you can put the coat down
nobody in here needs you to be anywhere

[Verse 2 — vibraphone in, very soft. Nothing else changes.]
You want me to tell you you haven't missed it.
Not pretty — you'd not believe pretty. That.
That there's still a queue and you're in it
and forty years didn't disqualify you from anything.

And you haven't. Missed it. I'd know, I've been here throughout.
People come in here at twenty-two and miss it entirely.
You've spent the lot on other people's evenings
and turned up to your own without a plan.

Your hands have been in your sleeves since you sat down.
Your eyes have done the room twice and me once, quickly,
and then the floor, and then me again, and stayed a bit longer.
There. That's the whole of it. That's all anybody ever does.

[Hook — identical words. Do not change them. The verse changed them for you.]
sit on my knee
you got dressed for this and somebody ought to say so
you're not late
you're just the last one to be told
and you can put the coat down
nobody in here needs you to be anywhere

[The near-yes — everything drops. Brushes and one muted trumpet, very quiet. She goes closer, warmer, softer, until the song appears to arrive.]
Closer.
Closer than that.
Close enough to smell the hairdresser's still on you.
Sit on my knee, pretty lady. You'll not need to be asked twice again.

[She stands, and puts her glass down where she was sitting, and leaves it. The band comes back in underneath her before anyone has worked out what happened.]

[Final hook — one word changed. Low, warm, walking away, hips last. Second voice half a beat behind on the last line only, and it is also hers.]
sit on my knee
you got dressed for this and somebody ought to say so
you're not late
you're just the last one to be told
and you can put the coat down
nobody in here needs you to be anywhere tonight

[Outro — the trumpet takes a phrase and doesn't finish it. Band plays on for another table. No resolving chord, no ending.]
