[Verse 1 — noir big band held right down. Muted brass, low strings, upright bass, brushed kit. Voice close, low, warm, behind the beat, never belting. She is answering something that was never said out loud.]
They told me it was too much skin.
Nineteen eighty-one. A letter. Two paragraphs.
So I made it more.
Same room, same Tuesday, six inches of difference.

Nobody said a word about it after.
That's the bit nobody believes.
They'd have had it off me in a minute
if a single one of them had wanted it gone.

[Pre-hook — brass steps in one notch, strings underneath, bass walks. Small lift. Not a build.]
Your phone's been face-down since I sat down.
I noticed. I notice everything. It's the job.

[Hook — barely bigger. Low, flat, warm, unhurried.]
stay for the second set
there's nothing on after and you've got no train to catch
I've been complained about by better than you'll ever meet
and I'm still here, and the letter isn't
you'd have stood up for me
you'd have stood up so beautifully

[Verse 2 — vibraphone and one muted trombone added. Strings hold. Nothing else changes.]
You want me to say you'd have been the one.
Not the evening — that. That's the order.
The one at the back who'd have said something.
Who'd have written his own letter. Two paragraphs.

And you would. I believe you. That's not a kindness,
that's the trouble with you, you'd have meant it.
Forty years of people meaning it at me.
I'd rather one of them had come to the show.

Your feet found mine under the table twice
and apologised twice, which is once too many.
Your eyes did the calves on the way in and pretended not to.
Go on, do it properly. It's a very good dress.

[Hook — identical words. Do not change them. The verse changed them for you.]
stay for the second set
there's nothing on after and you've got no train to catch
I've been complained about by better than you'll ever meet
and I'm still here, and the letter isn't
you'd have stood up for me
you'd have stood up so beautifully

[The near-yes — the whole band drops out. Brushes and one muted trumpet, very quiet. She goes closer, warmer, softer, until the song appears to arrive.]
Closer.
Closer than that.
Close enough to smell the dry ice still on me.
Nobody's ever had to stand up for me. That's the whole trick.

[She takes her earrings off the table, one and then the other, and stands. The band comes back in underneath her — brass first — before he has worked out what he was told.]

[Final hook — one word changed. Low, amused, entirely warm, walking away. Hips are the last thing anyone sees.]
stay for the second set
there's nothing on after and you've got no train to catch
I've been complained about by better than you'll ever meet
and I'm still here, and the letter isn't
you'd have stood up for me
you'd have looked so beautiful doing it

[Outro — the trumpet takes a phrase and doesn't finish it. Band plays on for another table. No resolving chord, no ending.]
