[Verse 1 — band held right down. Brushed kit, upright bass, piano, muted brass at the back of the room. Voice close, low, warm, behind the beat. She is answering something that was just said very quietly.]
Say that again.
No — not louder. Just again.
You got it out once. That's the hard one.
The second time is free.

You've been holding that since the coats.
I watched you not say it through two drinks and a story
about somebody's wedding
that I don't think happened.

[Pre-hook — brass steps in one notch, bass walks. Small lift. Not a build.]
You didn't look at my mouth once all night.
That's how I knew.

[Hook — barely bigger. Low, flat, warm, unhurried.]
say it again
nobody at this table's keeping score
never's not a small word
and I'm not going to make it one
plenty of people have been sure about me
never licked

[Verse 2 — vibraphone and one clarinet added. Nothing else changes.]
You want somebody to tell you it's allowed.
Not tonight — allowed. The whole of it.
That's the order, and I knew it at the door,
and I'd have poured it for free.

Your hands have been busy all evening.
The glass, the ring, the edge of the table, the ring.
And your eyes went everywhere except the one place,
which is its own kind of looking. I don't mind it.

[Hook — identical words. Do not change them. The verse changed them for you.]
say it again
nobody at this table's keeping score
never's not a small word
and I'm not going to make it one
plenty of people have been sure about me


[The near-yes — the whole band drops out. Brushes and one muted trumpet, very quiet. She goes closer, warmer, softer, until the song appears to arrive.]
Closer.
Closer than that.
Close enough to smell the rain still on your coat.
It's allowed. It was always allowed.

[She puts the check face-down, takes her glass with her, and stands. The band comes back in underneath her before she has worked out what happened.]

[Final hook — one word changed. Low, amused, entirely warm, walking away. Hips are the last thing anyone sees.]
say it again
nobody at this table's keeping score
never's not a small word
and I didn't make it one
plenty of people have been sure about me

say it to somebody
